Explore Oslo: Popular Activities

Discover the best of Norway's capital

Viking Ship Museum

Step back in time and explore ancient Viking ships and artifacts.

Discover three of the world’s best-preserved Viking ships in Oslo! The museum houses the stunning Oseberg, the adventure-ready Gokstad, and the practical Tune—each offering a unique window into Viking life and craftsmanship.

  • Oseberg Ship: An intricately carved burial ship for two high-status women, featuring treasures like tapestries, sleds, and ceremonial items.
  • Gokstad Ship: Built for exploration, this larger ship highlights Viking seafaring skills, found with a chieftain’s burial goods.
  • Tune Ship: A simpler vessel for everyday fjord travel, giving insight into daily Viking life.

Visit the Viking Ship Museum for an unforgettable journey into Norse history!

Holmenkollen Ski Jump

Visit the iconic ski jump and enjoy panoramic views of Oslo.

Experience Norway’s famous Holmenkollen Ski Jump, one of the world's oldest ski jumps and a landmark in Oslo’s skyline.

  • History: Built in 1892, Holmenkollen has hosted numerous world-class ski events.
  • Design: A modern marvel, blending engineering and aesthetics to offer panoramic city and fjord views.
  • Experience: Visit the top to enjoy breathtaking views, or explore the Ski Museum and learn about Norway’s skiing history.

A must-visit for adrenaline and history enthusiasts alike!

Oslo Opera House

Walk on the roof of this architectural marvel and enjoy a performance.

Discover the architectural gem of Oslo! The Oslo Opera House is renowned for its unique design and invites visitors to walk on its sloping marble roof for scenic city views.

  • Architecture: An award-winning design resembling a glacier rising from the water.
  • Experience: Enjoy a performance, or explore the rooftop for panoramic views of Oslo and the fjord.
  • Art and Culture: Home to the Norwegian National Opera and Ballet, with vibrant events year-round.

A must-see for architecture lovers and art enthusiasts alike!
