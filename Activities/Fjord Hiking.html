Explore Norway: Fjord Hiking Adventures

Discover breathtaking trails along Norway's majestic fjords

Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock)

Hike to one of Norway's most famous cliffs overlooking Lysefjorden.

Preikestolen, or Pulpit Rock, is one of Norway's most iconic hiking destinations. This flat-topped cliff rises 604 meters above Lysefjorden, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Distance: 8 km round trip
  • Duration: 4-5 hours
  • Best Time: June to September

The hike to Preikestolen is well-marked and suitable for most fitness levels. Remember to bring water, snacks, and weather-appropriate clothing.

Kjeragbolten

Dare to stand on a boulder wedged between two cliffs above Lysefjorden.

Kjeragbolten is a boulder trapped between two rocks above a 984-meter deep abyss. This thrilling hike offers stunning views of Lysefjorden and the chance to step onto the famous boulder.

  • Difficulty: Challenging
  • Distance: 12 km round trip
  • Duration: 6-8 hours
  • Best Time: June to September

This hike involves some steep and exposed sections. Proper hiking boots and caution are essential. The views and experience are unforgettable!

Trolltunga

Hike to the "Troll's Tongue," a rock formation jutting out over Lake Ringedalsvatnet.

Trolltunga, or "Troll's Tongue," is a dramatic rock formation extending horizontally out of a mountain about 700 meters above Lake Ringedalsvatnet. The hike offers spectacular scenery of the surrounding fjords and mountains.

  • Difficulty: Challenging
  • Distance: 28 km round trip
  • Duration: 10-12 hours
  • Best Time: June to September

This is a long and demanding hike requiring good physical condition. Start early and be prepared with proper gear, food, and water. The views from Trolltunga are truly unforgettable!
